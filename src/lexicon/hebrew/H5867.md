# H5867 עֵילָם / עילם

## ʻÊylâm

_(ay-lawm' | ay-LAWM | ay-LAHM)_

### Definition

proper locative noun, proper masculine noun; eternity; or עוֹלָם; (Ezra 10:2; Jeremiah 49:36), probably from [[H5956]]; hidden, i.e., distant; Elam, a son of Shem and his descendants, with their country; also of six Israelites; Elam.

- a Korhite Levite in the time of David (noun proper masculine)
- a chief man of the tribe of Benjamin (noun proper masculine)
- ancestor of a family of exiles who returned with Zerubbabel (noun proper masculine)
- a chief of the people who signed the covenant with Nehemiah (noun proper masculine)
- another ancestor of another family of exiles who returned with Zerubbabel (noun proper masculine)
- a priest who assisted at the dedication of the wall of Jerusalem in the time of Nehemiah (noun proper masculine)
- another head of a family of returning exiles (noun proper masculine)
- a province east of Babylon and northeast of the lower Tigris (noun proper locative)
